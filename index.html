Rules

  • All participants must adhere to the hackathon theme and focus on creating innovative solutions within the specified problem statement.
  • Teams must consist of the designated number of members (if applicable) and should not exceed the maximum team size.
  • Participants must register for the hackathon before the specified deadline.
  • Teams should assign a team lead and ensure effective communication and collaboration throughout the event.
  • Each team must submit their project before the designated submission deadline.
  • All code and assets used in the project must be original, or proper attribution should be given for any open-source or third-party components.
  • Participants are expected to maintain a professional and respectful attitude towards fellow participants, organizers, and judges.
  • Plagiarism or any form of cheating will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • Participants must follow the guidelines provided by the organizers and comply with any technical or logistical requirements.
  • Teams are responsible for their own equipment, software, and internet connectivity during the hackathon.
  • The decisions of the judges and organizers are final and binding.
  • Participants must not engage in any malicious activities, such as hacking, unauthorized access, or disruptive behavior.
  • All participants must respect the privacy and intellectual property rights of others.
  • Participants are responsible for their own safety and well-being during the hackathon.
  • Any violation of the hackathon rules may result in penalties or disqualification, as determined by the organizers.
  • By participating in the hackathon, participants agree to allow the organizers to use their names, project details, and media coverage for promotional purposes.
